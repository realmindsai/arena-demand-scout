Geographic Unit: Statistical Area Level 2 (SA2)

All suburb-level analysis uses SA2 (Statistical Area Level 2) regions from the Australian Statistical Geography Standard (ASGS) 2021 edition. SA2s are the finest geographic unit for which the ABS publishes population by single year of age. There are approximately 2,470 SA2 regions across Australia, typically representing suburbs, towns, or rural districts with populations of 3,000–25,000 people.

SA2 boundaries are sourced from the ABS ArcGIS REST API with simplified geometry (maxAllowableOffset=0.01, ~1 km) for browser performance.

Accessibility Model: Two-Step Floating Catchment Area (2SFCA)

Raw "places per child" within an SA2 boundary is misleading: a suburb with zero centres but adjacent to a suburb with many centres may still have good childcare access. To account for cross-boundary catchment effects, we use the Two-Step Floating Catchment Area (2SFCA) method, a standard spatial accessibility measure from health geography.

Step 1 (Supply ratio): For each SA2 j with childcare supply, compute Rj = Sj / Σ (Pk · w(dkj)) for all SA2s k within catchment radius.

Step 2 (Accessibility index): For each SA2 i, sum Ai = Σ (Rj · w(dij)) for all supply SA2s j within catchment radius.

Distance decay: Gaussian function w(d) = exp(−d² / β²) where β = radius/2. Catchment radius = 5 km (~15 min urban drive).

The resulting accessibility index (Ai) represents the effective places-per-child available to each SA2, accounting for nearby supply and competing demand from neighbouring regions. Lower values indicate underserved areas with higher expansion opportunity.

Reference: Luo, W. & Wang, F. (2003). "Measures of spatial accessibility to health care in a GIS environment: synthesis and a case study in the Chicago region." Environment and Planning B: Planning and Design, 30(6), 865–884.

Opportunity Scoring

Each SA2 region receives a composite opportunity score (0–100) based on percentile ranking across three factors:

Factor Weight Rationale
Children per km² 40% Density of demand (urban concentration)
Population aged 0–4 30% Raw market size
Supply gap (2SFCA) 30% Catchment-adjusted undersupply (inverted accessibility)

Percentile ranking is used rather than min-max normalisation to ensure even distribution across ~2,500 SA2 regions. The final score is: 0.4 × density_pctile + 0.3 × pop_pctile + 0.3 × supply_gap_pctile, scaled to 0–100.
